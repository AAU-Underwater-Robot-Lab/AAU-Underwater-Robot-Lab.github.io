ACOMAR - Auto Compact Marine Growth Remover
Type: Industrial Research Project
Period: 2018 - 2023

ACOMAR is a innovation partnership led by SubC Partner, Aalborg University, Siemens Gamesa Renewable Energy, TotalEnergies Danmark, Mati2ilt, Sihm Højtryk and EIVA that has produced a compact, semi-autonomous underwater robot capable of both inspecting and removing marine biofouling from offshore wind-turbine foundations and oil-and-gas platforms. Deployed directly from the structure under maintenance, the ring-shaped unit manoeuvres with integrated thrusters and sensor-based controls while applying high-pressure water jets that dislodge mussels and algae without abrasive contact, reducing environmental impact and restoring clear access for structural inspections. Because the system operates with a crew of only one to three technicians and does not rely on large service vessels, it markedly lowers logistical overheads. Field modelling indicates that widespread adoption could trim North Sea cleaning costs by 30 - 40 percent, extend structural fatigue life and deliver measurable reductions in the levelised cost of offshore energy, positioning Danish robotics expertise for new export opportunities.
